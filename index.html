How I Work

A few things I try to live by.

Not rules I've mastered — just the things I keep coming back to.

01

The team gets the credit

When a project lands well, it's because the people around me showed up. My job is to make their work a little easier and then get out of their way.

02

Listen before I lead

I assume the people doing the work understand it better than I do. So I ask a lot of questions, learn, and only then figure out how I can help.

03

Small promises, kept

I'd rather quietly under-promise and deliver than impress in a meeting and let someone down later. Trust is built in the boring, reliable moments.

04

Stay calm, stay kind

When things go sideways — and they do — I try to steady the room instead of adding to the noise. People do their best work when they feel safe, not stressed.

Calm in the chaos, driven in the quiet. I hold high standards and sweat the details — but I'm the first to hand the spotlight to my team. If we win, it's because they showed up; my job is to clear the path and keep everyone moving.
